## ✏️ Essay 17 - Living Alone Trend

### 💡 Argument 1: Health/Well-being (Freedom/Mind)
**Topic:** Living alone gives freedom and reduces stress.

1.  **Pros (Of Living Alone):**
    *   You have complete privacy in your own home.
    *   You can design the house exactly how you like it.
2.  **Cons (Of Living Alone):**
    *   It can be very lonely on weekends or holidays.
    *   There is nobody to help you if you get sick suddenly.
3.  **Reasons (Why choose it?):**
    *   Modern people value independence more than before.
    *   Avoiding arguments about cleaning or noise is peaceful.
4.  **Phenomena (What we see):**
    *   Young professionals renting studio apartments in the city.
    *   People cooking dinner for themselves while watching Netflix.
5.  **Effects (On health):**
    *   Less stress from bad roommates = better sleep.
    *   Strong self-confidence and self-reliance skills.
6.  **Solutions (To avoid loneliness):**
    *   Join clubs or gyms to meet people outside the house.
    *   Invite friends over for dinner regularly.
7.  **Examples (Concrete instances):**
    *   **A university student** can study quietly at 2 AM without disturbing anyone.
    *   **A divorced man** feels happy painting his walls red because nobody can stop him.

### 💡 Argument 2: Money/Economy (Spending)
**Topic:** Living alone increases consumer spending (Good for business).

1.  **Pros (For Economy):**
    *   More houses are built, creating jobs for builders.
    *   More furniture and appliances are sold.
2.  **Cons (For Individual Wallet):**
    *   Rent and bills are much higher for one person.
    *   It is harder to save money for the future.
3.  **Reasons (Why spending increases?):**
    *   Every single person needs their own fridge, TV, and sofa.
    *   You cannot split the electricity bill with anyone.
4.  **Phenomena (What we see):**
    *   Shops selling "single portion" meals.
    *   Apartment buildings full of one-bedroom units.
5.  **Effects (On market):**
    *   The housing market becomes very expensive.
    *   Companies make more profit from selling household goods.
6.  **Solutions (To manage cost):**
    *   Learn to cook at home instead of eating out.
    *   Buy second-hand furniture to save money.
7.  **Examples (Concrete instances):**
    *   **IKEA** sells thousands of small sofas designed for single-person studios.
    *   **Supermarkets** now sell half-loaves of bread for people living alone.

## ✏️ Essay 18 - Wealthy Countries and Refugees

### 💡 Argument 1: Society/Humanity (Moral Duty)
**Topic:** Rich countries must accept refugees to save lives.

1.  **Pros (Of Accepting):**
    *   It saves innocent families from death or torture.
    *   The country gains a reputation for being kind and moral.
2.  **Cons (Of Rejection):**
    *   People die at the border or in the sea.
    *   The wealthy country looks selfish and cruel.
3.  **Reasons (Why accept?):**
    *   Refugees did not choose to leave; they were forced by war.
    *   Rich nations have enough food and money to share.
4.  **Phenomena (What we see):**
    *   News reports of boats full of desperate people.
    *   Refugee camps with tents and little food.
5.  **Effects (On society):**
    *   A more diverse and multicultural society.
    *   Citizens feel proud to help others in need.
6.  **Solutions (How to help):**
    *   Government should provide temporary housing and language lessons.
    *   Charities can collect clothes and toys for refugee children.
7.  **Examples (Concrete instances):**
    *   **Germany** accepted over 1 million refugees from Syria in 2015.
    *   **A local church** donates warm coats to refugee families in winter.

### 💡 Argument 2: Money/Economy (Long-term Benefit)
**Topic:** Refugees contribute to the economy (Jobs/Tax).

1.  **Pros (Of New Workers):**
    *   Refugees are often young and willing to work hard.
    *   They fill jobs that locals do not want (cleaning, farming).
2.  **Cons (Of Initial Cost):**
    *   The government must pay for their food and housing at first.
    *   Unemployment might rise slightly in the short term.
3.  **Reasons (Why beneficial?):**
    *   Many refugees start their own small businesses quickly.
    *   They pay taxes for many years after they find work.
4.  **Phenomena (What we see):**
    *   Immigrant families running successful restaurants or shops.
    *   Doctors or engineers from other countries working in hospitals.
5.  **Effects (On economy):**
    *   Economic growth due to a larger workforce.
    *   Cultural enrichment leads to tourism and trade.
6.  **Solutions (To speed up):**
    *   Make it easier for refugees to get work permits.
    *   Recognize their university degrees from their home country.
7.  **Examples (Concrete instances):**
    *   **Steve Jobs**, the founder of Apple, was the son of a Syrian immigrant.
    *   **Turkish doner kebabs** have become a huge business in Germany, employing thousands.

## ✏️ Essay 19 - Government Funding for Artists

### 💡 Argument 1: Society/Culture (Art Value)
**Topic:** Art improves culture and happiness.

1.  **Pros (Of Funding Art):**
    *   Museums and galleries stay open for free.
    *   Cities look beautiful with statues and public art.
2.  **Cons (Of Cutting Art Funds):**
    *   Artists cannot survive and must stop creating.
    *   The country becomes boring and has no cultural identity.
3.  **Reasons (Why fund?):**
    *   Art tells the story of the nation's history.
    *   It attracts tourists who spend money in the city.
4.  **Phenomena (What we see):**
    *   Street festivals with music and dancing.
    *   Children learning to paint in community centers.
5.  **Effects (On society):**
    *   People feel proud of their local culture.
    *   Mental health improves through enjoying beauty.
6.  **Solutions (To support):**
    *   Government grants for young artists.
    *   Public competitions for new sculptures or murals.
7.  **Examples (Concrete instances):**
    *   **The Eiffel Tower** in Paris is a piece of art that brings millions of tourists.
    *   **Shakespeare's plays** are still performed today because the government supports theatres.

### 💡 Argument 2: Health/Priorities (Opportunity Cost)
**Topic:** Money is better spent on healthcare (Saving lives).

1.  **Pros (Of Healthcare Funding):**
    *   More doctors and nurses can be hired.
    *   Wait times for surgery become shorter.
2.  **Cons (Of Art Funding):**
    *   Every dollar for art is a dollar taken from a sick patient.
    *   People might die because the hospital lacks equipment.
3.  **Reasons (Why prioritize health?):**
    *   Life and death are more important than entertainment.
    *   Health is a basic human right; art is a luxury.
4.  **Phenomena (What we see):**
    *   Old hospitals with broken beds and not enough staff.
    *   Patients waiting in the corridor for a room.
5.  **Effects (Of spending on health):**
    *   A healthier population that can work longer.
    *   Fewer unnecessary deaths from treatable diseases.
6.  **Solutions (To decide):**
    *   Use lottery money to fund arts, not tax money.
    *   Ask rich companies to sponsor art galleries.
7.  **Examples (Concrete instances):**
    *   **During Covid-19**, governments stopped funding concerts to pay for vaccines.
    *   **A cancer hospital** needs money for an MRI machine more than a city needs a new statue.

## ✏️ Essay 20 - Cars in Developing Countries

### 💡 Argument 1: Society/Environment (Pollution)
**Topic:** More cars cause pollution and health problems.

1.  **Pros (Of Cars - counter-point):**
    *   They are convenient for going to work or shopping.
    *   They show that the family is getting richer.
2.  **Cons (Of Pollution):**
    *   The air becomes grey and smells of petrol.
    *   People get asthma and lung cancer.
3.  **Reasons (Why problematic?):**
    *   Old cars in developing countries have bad engines.
    *   Cities were built for walking, not for millions of cars.
4.  **Phenomena (What we see):**
    *   Traffic jams that last for 3 hours every morning.
    *   People wearing masks just to walk on the street.
5.  **Effects (On city):**
    *   Doctors see more children with breathing problems.
    *   Productivity drops because workers are stuck in traffic.
6.  **Solutions (To reduce cars):**
    *   Ban cars from the city center on certain days.
    *   Make parking very expensive to discourage driving.
7.  **Examples (Concrete instances):**
    *   **In Beijing**, on some days you cannot see the sun because of smog from cars.
    *   **Motorcycles** in Vietnam create loud noise and dirty smoke all day.

### 💡 Argument 2: Society/Transport (Solution)
**Topic:** Public Transport is the solution.

1.  **Pros (Of Trains/Buses):**
    *   One train carries 1000 people (removes 800 cars).
    *   It is cheaper for the passengers than buying petrol.
2.  **Cons (Of Building It):**
    *   Subway systems are extremely expensive to build.
    *   Construction takes many years and blocks roads.
3.  **Reasons (Why solution?):**
    *   It is the only way to move millions of people quickly.
    *   Electric trains produce zero smoke in the city.
4.  **Phenomena (What we see):**
    *   Clean, modern metro stations in big cities like Tokyo.
    *   Dedicated lanes for buses so they don't get stuck.
5.  **Effects (On environment):**
    *   Air quality improves significantly.
    *   The city becomes quieter and nicer to live in.
6.  **Solutions (To encourage use):**
    *   Make ticket prices very low.
    *   Ensure trains are safe, clean, and on time.
7.  **Examples (Concrete instances):**
    *   **The Tube in London** carries 5 million people a day, keeping cars off the streets.
    *   **Japan's Bullet Train** is so fast that people prefer it over driving.

## ✏️ Essay 21 - Teenagers and Smartphones

### 💡 Argument 1: Health/Safety (GPS & Contact)
**Topic:** Phones improve safety for teenagers.

1.  **Pros (Of Safety):**
    *   Parents can call their child if they are late.
    *   The child can call police instantly in an emergency.
2.  **Cons (Of Tracking):**
    *   Teenagers feel their parents do not trust them.
    *   They might turn off the GPS to hide.
3.  **Reasons (Why useful?):**
    *   The world can be dangerous at night.
    *   Plans change quickly (e.g., missed bus).
4.  **Phenomena (What we see):**
    *   Checking "Find My Friends" app to see location.
    *   Texting "I am on my way home" to Mom.
5.  **Effects (On parents):**
    *   Less worry and anxiety when the child is out.
    *   More freedom given to the child because of safety link.
6.  **Solutions (To balance):**
    *   Agree on rules: "Start tracking only after 10 PM."
    *   Teach children to keep phones charged at all times.
7.  **Examples (Concrete instances):**
    *   **A girl** who missed the last bus can call her dad to pick her up safely.
    *   **Parents** use apps to make sure their son arrived at school on time.

### 💡 Argument 2: Society/Communication (Social Skills)
**Topic:** Phones hurt face-to-face communication skills.

1.  **Pros (Of Online Chat - counter-point):**
    *   Ideally, you can talk to friends anytime.
    *   Shy people feel braver typing than speaking.
2.  **Cons (Of Phone Addiction):**
    *   Eye contact disappears during conversation.
    *   People forget how to read body language and tone.
3.  **Reasons (Why bad?):**
    *   Screens are addictive and brighter than reality.
    *   Typing is easier than the awkwardness of real talk.
4.  **Phenomena (What we see):**
    *   A group of friends at a cafe, all looking down.
    *   Silence at the dinner table because of phones.
5.  **Effects (On future):**
    *   Difficulty in job interviews ( cannot speak confidently).
    *   Weaker relationships with family and friends.
6.  **Solutions (To fix):**
    *   "No Phone Zone" at the dinner table.
    *   Leave the phone in another room when studying or talking.
7.  **Examples (Concrete instances):**
    *   **Teenagers** often text each other even when sitting in the same room.
    *   **Many young people** feel panic if they have to make a real phone call to a stranger.

## ✏️ Essay 22 - Climate Change vs Nuclear Weapons

### 💡 Argument 1: Society/Tech (Climate Solvable)
**Topic:** Climate change is a slow threat that technology can fix.

1.  **Pros (Of Modern Tech):**
    *   Solar panels are becoming cheaper than coal.
    *   Electric cars are cleaner than petrol cars.
2.  **Cons (Of Slow Action):**
    *   If we wait too long, it might be too late.
    *   Some countries refuse to change their factories.
3.  **Reasons (Why solvable?):**
    *   Humans are smart and inventive when in danger.
    *   We already have the science to stop global warming.
4.  **Phenomena (What we see):**
    *   Wind farms in the ocean generating power.
    *   Recycling bins in every home and office.
5.  **Effects (If successful):**
    *   The planet stays cool and safe for humans.
    *   New "green" jobs are created for engineers.
6.  **Solutions (To speed up):**
    *   Government should pay for solar panels on roofs.
    *   Ban plastic bags and single-use items.
7.  **Examples (Concrete instances):**
    *   **Tesla** makes electric cars that do not pollute the air at all.
    *   **The Netherlands** uses wind energy to power millions of homes.

### 💡 Argument 2: Health/Death (Nuclear Threat)
**Topic:** Nuclear weapons are an instant, unstoppable threat.

1.  **Pros (Of Nuclear Power - counter-point):**
    *   Nuclear energy (not bombs) is clean electricity.
    *   Weapons might stop big wars because of fear (deterrence).
2.  **Cons (Of Nuclear War):**
    *   Total destruction of cities in seconds.
    *   Radiation sickness kills survivors slowly and painfully.
3.  **Reasons (Why bigger threat?):**
    *   You cannot fix a nuclear explosion with technology.
    *   One mistake by a leader can end the world today.
4.  **Phenomena (What we see):**
    *   News about countries testing missiles.
    *   Tension between powerful nations like USA and Russia.
5.  **Effects (Of war):**
    *   Nuclear Winter (sun blocked by smoke, crops die).
    *   Human civilization ends permanently.
6.  **Solutions (To prevent):**
    *   International treaties to reduce the number of bombs.
    *   Diplomacy and talking instead of fighting.
7.  **Examples (Concrete instances):**
    *   **Hiroshima** was destroyed by one small bomb in 1945; modern bombs are 1000 times stronger.
    *   **Movies** show that if a nuclear war starts, nobody can hide or survive.
