## ✏️ Essay 1 - Smoking Ban in Public Places

### 💡 Argument 1: Health/Well-being (Safety/Body)
**Topic:** Banning smoking to protect non-smokers (passive smoking).

1.  **Pros (Benefits of banning):**
    *   It protects the lungs of people who do not smoke, especially children.
    *   It creates cleaner and fresher air in public places like parks and restaurants.
2.  **Cons (Disadvantages for smokers):**
    *   Smokers feel they have lost their freedom to enjoy a cigarette.
    *   Some businesses (like bars) might lose customers who want to smoke inside.
3.  **Reasons (Why ban it?):**
    *   Smoke contains dangerous chemicals that cause cancer.
    *   Non-smokers did not choose to breathe this toxic air.
4.  **Phenomena (What we see):**
    *   We often see children coughing when they walk past a smoker.
    *   Restaurants sometimes smell of old smoke, which is unpleasant.
5.  **Effects (Results of the ban):**
    *   The rate of lung cancer and heart disease will go down.
    *   More families will visit public places because they feel safe.
6.  **Solutions (How to implement):**
    *   The government must pass strict laws to punish people who smoke in public.
    *   Put up clear "No Smoking" signs in all parks and bus stops.
7.  **Examples (Concrete instances):**
    *   **In Australia**, after banning smoking in restaurants, fewer waiters got lung cancer.
    *   **A mother** can now take her baby to a café without worrying about dirty smoke.

### 💡 Argument 2: Money/Economy (Government Spending)
**Topic:** Smoking costs the government a lot of money (healthcare).

1.  **Pros (Financial benefits of quitting):**
    *   The government saves millions of dollars on hospital bills.
    *   This money can be used for schools or building better roads.
2.  **Cons (Economic losses):**
    *   Tobacco companies will sell fewer cigarettes and pay less tax to the government.
    *   Tobacco farmers might lose their jobs and income.
3.  **Reasons (Why is it expensive?):**
    *   Treating long-term diseases like cancer requires expensive medicine and machines.
    *   Smokers take more sick days, which hurts productivity.
4.  **Phenomena (What we see):**
    *   Public hospitals are full of patients with breathing problems.
    *   The health budget is extremely high every year due to lifestyle diseases.
5.  **Effects (Financial results):**
    *   Taxes for everyone might go down if healthcare costs drop.
    *   The country becomes richer because the workforce is healthier.
6.  **Solutions (How to fix):**
    *   Increase the price of cigarettes so fewer people buy them.
    *   Make smokers pay for their own private health insurance.
7.  **Examples (Concrete instances):**
    *   **The UK government** spends billions of pounds every year treating smokers in hospitals.
    *   **A taxi driver** who smokes might get sick and stop paying taxes for many months.

## ✏️ Essay 2 - Competition vs Cooperation

### 💡 Argument 1: Health/Well-being (Mind/Stress)
**Topic:** Competition causes stress; Cooperation causes less stress.

1.  **Pros (Of Cooperation/Less Competition):**
    *   Children feel happier and less anxious about losing.
    *   It builds a friendly environment in the classroom.
2.  **Cons (Of No Competition):**
    *   Children might become lazy if there is no pressure to win.
    *   They might not learn how to handle failure in real life.
3.  **Reasons (Why competition hurts?):**
    *   Young minds are fragile and can lose confidence easily.
    *   Comparing scores makes some students feel stupid/useless.
4.  **Phenomena (What we see):**
    *   Students crying after getting a low test score.
    *   Classmates refusing to help each other because they want to be the best.
5.  **Effects (Long-term impact):**
    *   High stress can lead to mental health problems like depression.
    *   Students might hate school and stop trying to learn.
6.  **Solutions (How to balance):**
    *   Teachers should praise effort, not just the final score.
    *   Encourage team sports where winning requires working together.
7.  **Examples (Concrete instances):**
    *   **A young boy** who always loses in races might think he is no good at anything.
    *   **In Finnish schools**, students rarely take exams, so they are less stressed than Asian students.

### 💡 Argument 2: Money/Economy (Jobs/Income)
**Topic:** Cooperation is better for future jobs and business.

1.  **Pros (Of Cooperation Skills):**
    *   Employees work faster when they help each other.
    *   Companies solve problems better with teamwork.
2.  **Cons (Of Teamwork Focus):**
    *   Some lazy workers might hide behind the hard work of others.
    *   Decisions can take longer because everyone must agree.
3.  **Reasons (Why business needs it?):**
    *   Modern projects (like building software) are too big for one person.
    *   Good relationships with coworkers make the office more productive.
4.  **Phenomena (What we see):**
    *   Job interviews often ask: "Can you work in a team?"
    *   Successful companies like Google have open offices to encourage talking.
5.  **Effects (Career results):**
    *   People with good social skills get promoted to manager faster.
    *   Businesses make more profit through efficient collaboration.
6.  **Solutions (How to teach it):**
    *   Schools should give more group projects and presentations.
    *   Parents should teach children to share toys and help with housework.
7.  **Examples (Concrete instances):**
    *   **Engineers at NASA** must work together to launch a rocket safely.
    *   **A shop manager** relies on their staff to keep the store clean and customers happy.

## ✏️ Essay 3 - Childcare: Family vs Centre

### 💡 Argument 1: Society/Relationship (Family/Culture)
**Topic:** Grandparents provide love and cultural values.

1.  **Pros (Of Family Care):**
    *   Grandparents love the child more than any paid worker.
    *   The child learns family traditions and language naturally.
2.  **Cons (Of Family Care):**
    *   Grandparents might spoil the child (give too much candy/freedom).
    *   Their knowledge of education might be old-fashioned.
3.  **Reasons (Why choose family?):**
    *   Parents trust their own parents more than strangers.
    *   It is usually free, which helps the family save money.
4.  **Phenomena (What we see):**
    *   Grandmothers telling old stories to toddlers.
    *   Children feeling very safe and happy at home.
5.  **Effects (On the child):**
    *   The child develops a strong connection with their relatives.
    *   They might be shy when they first go to school later.
6.  **Solutions (How to improve):**
    *   Grandparents should also read books to children, not just play.
    *   Parents should set clear rules for grandparents to follow.
7.  **Examples (Concrete instances):**
    *   **A grandmother** in Italy teaches her grandson how to cook traditional pasta sauce.
    *   **Working parents** save $1000 a month because grandma looks after the baby for free.

### 💡 Argument 2: Society/Relationship (Education/Friends)
**Topic:** Childcare centres provide education and social skills.

1.  **Pros (Of Centre Care):**
    *   Teachers are trained to teach reading, writing, and art.
    *   Children learn how to make friends and share toys.
2.  **Cons (Of Centre Care):**
    *   It can be very expensive for the parents.
    *   Result: Children might get sick often from other kids.
3.  **Reasons (Why choose centres?):**
    *   Professional teachers know how to develop a child's brain.
    *   Staying at home alone can be boring for an active child.
4.  **Phenomena (What we see):**
    *   Classrooms full of educational toys and colorful books.
    *   Groups of children singing songs or playing games together.
5.  **Effects (On the child):**
    *   They are more prepared for primary school.
    *   They become more independent and confident socially.
6.  **Solutions (How to balance):**
    *   Send children to a centre for half the day, and family for the rest.
    *   The government should make childcare cheaper for poor families.
7.  **Examples (Concrete instances):**
    *   **A shy boy** learns to speak confidently after playing with 20 other kids at a centre.
    *   **Teachers** notice that children from daycare already know their ABCs before starting school.

## ✏️ Essay 4 - Simpler Tech vs Modern Tech

### 💡 Argument 1: Health/Well-being (Safety/Life Expectancy)
**Topic:** Modern technology (medicine) saves lives.

1.  **Pros (Of Medical Tech):**
    *   Doctors can cure diseases that used to kill people.
    *   Surgery creates less pain and recovery is faster.
2.  **Cons (Of Medical Tech):**
    *   New treatments are often very expensive.
    *   People might rely on pills instead of having a healthy lifestyle.
3.  **Reasons (Why it's better now?):**
    *   We have scanners (MRI) that can see inside the body.
    *   Scientists have created vaccines for many viruses.
4.  **Phenomena (What we see):**
    *   Old people living until 90 or 100 years old.
    *   Hospitals using robots to help with difficult operations.
5.  **Effects (On society):**
    *   Families spend more years together with their grandparents.
    *   The population is getting older because fewer people die young.
6.  **Solutions (How to share benefits):**
    *   Rich countries should share medical tech with poor countries.
    *   Governments should pay for essential medicines for everyone.
7.  **Examples (Concrete instances):**
    *   ** Vaccines** saved millions of people from dying of Covid-19 recently.
    *   **A cancer patient** can now survive for 20 years thanks to new drugs.

### 💡 Argument 2: Money/Economy (Business/Income)
**Topic:** Technology helps business and creates wealth.

1.  **Pros (Of Business Tech):**
    *   Work is much faster using computers and the internet.
    *   Companies can sell products to customers all over the world.
2.  **Cons (Of Business Tech):**
    *   Robots might take jobs away from factory workers.
    *   Employees feel stressed because they must answer emails 24/7.
3.  **Reasons (Why it helps income?):**
    *   One person can do the work of ten people with a computer.
    *   Online shops act as a window to the global market.
4.  **Phenomena (What we see):**
    *   People working from home using Zoom or email.
    *   Small businesses selling handmade goods on Instagram.
5.  **Effects (On economy):**
    *   The world economy grows bigger every year.
    *   New types of jobs (like app developers) are created.
6.  **Solutions (To handle changes):**
    *   Workers need to learn new computer skills to stay employed.
    *   Companies should set rules to stop working after office hours.
7.  **Examples (Concrete instances):**
    *   **Amazon** became a huge company because the internet allows it to sell books globally.
    *   **A graphic designer** can work for a client in London while living in Bangkok.

## ✏️ Essay 5 - Media Impact

### 💡 Argument 1: Health/Well-being (Mind/Stress)
**Topic:** Bad news in media causes stress and fear.

1.  **Pros (Of knowing news - counter-point):**
    *   It is good to be informed about dangers (like a storm).
    *   We can learn about the world outside our city.
2.  **Cons (Of bad news):**
    *   It makes people feel anxious and sad all the time.
    *   It can cause panic (like buying too much toilet paper).
3.  **Reasons (Why is it stressful?):**
    *   News channels only show disasters to get more viewers.
    *   Human brains focus more on danger than happy things.
4.  **Phenomena (What we see):**
    *   Headlines about war, viruses, and accidents every day.
    *   People checking their phones for news 50 times a day.
5.  **Effects (On health):**
    *   Mental health problems like depression increase.
    *   People become afraid to leave their houses.
6.  **Solutions (How to fix):**
    *   Limit time spent watching the news (e.g., 30 mins a day).
    *   Media should also report positive and happy stories.
7.  **Examples (Concrete instances):**
    *   **During the pandemic**, watching the death count daily made many people depressed.
    *   **An old lady** might stop going out at night because TV says crime is everywhere.

### 💡 Argument 2: Society/Relationship (Crime/Safety)
**Topic:** Violence in media (movies/games) leads to crime.

1.  **Pros (Of Action Movies - counter-point):**
    *   They are exciting and entertaining to watch.
    *   They help people release stress by watching fighting.
2.  **Cons (Of Violence):**
    *   Young people might copy the violent actions they see.
    *   It makes people think hurting others is normal.
3.  **Reasons (Why it causes crime?):**
    *   Children cannot tell the difference between movies and real life.
    *   Heroes in movies often use violence to solve problems.
4.  **Phenomena (What we see):**
    *   Teenagers fighting in the street like movie characters.
    *   Video games where points are given for killing.
5.  **Effects (On society):**
    *   The crime rate in cities might go up.
    *   Schools become less safe for students.
6.  **Solutions (How to fix):**
    *   Parents must check the age limit on video games.
    *   Government should censor movies that differ too much violence.
7.  **Examples (Concrete instances):**
    *   **A specific school shooting** was blamed on the killer playing violent war games.
    *   **Parents** notice their son hitting his sister after watching a superhero fight movie.

## ✏️ Essay 6 - Money vs Job Satisfaction

### 💡 Argument 1: Health/Well-being (Happiness/Mind)
**Topic:** Happiness is more important than money.

1.  **Pros (Of choosing happiness):**
    *   You feel excited to go to work every morning.
    *   Your mental health stays strong and positive.
2.  **Cons (Of ignoring money):**
    *   You might not be able to buy luxuries or travel.
    *   You might worry about paying bills if the salary is low.
3.  **Reasons (Why happiness matters?):**
    *   We spend 8 hours a day at work; hating it is a waste of life.
    *   Stress from a bad job causes physical sickness (headaches).
4.  **Phenomena (What we see):**
    *   Rich bankers quitting their jobs to become teachers or artists.
    *   People smiling and laughing with colleagues in a happy office.
5.  **Effects (On life):**
    *   People live longer because they are less stressed.
    *   They are nicer to their family when they come home.
6.  **Solutions (How to achieve):**
    *   Choose a career based on passion, not just salary.
    *   Companies should focus on making the workplace fun.
7.  **Examples (Concrete instances):**
    *   **A lawyer** who was rich but depressed quit to become a gardener and is now happy.
    *   **Google offices** have slides and free food to make employees happy.

### 💡 Argument 2: Society/Relationship (Family Time)
**Topic:** Time with family is the main reason to stay/leave.

1.  **Pros (Of Family Time):**
    *   Parents build a strong relationship with their children.
    *   The marriage remains happy and stable.
2.  **Cons (Of Family Focus):**
    *   You might miss chances to get promoted at work.
    *   You might earn less money than workaholic colleagues.
3.  **Reasons (Why family first?):**
    *   Children grow up fast; you cannot get that time back.
    *   Family is the only support when you are old or sick.
4.  **Phenomena (What we see):**
    *   Fathers leaving the office at 5 PM sharp to pick up kids.
    *   People refusing overtime work on weekends.
5.  **Effects (On society):**
    *   Fewer divorces and broken families.
    *   Children grow up to be better citizens with good guidance.
6.  **Solutions (How to balance):**
    *   Companies should offer flexible working hours.
    *   Government should give more holidays to workers.
7.  **Examples (Concrete instances):**
    *   **A mother** rejected a high-paying job in another city because she didn't want to move her kids.
    *   **Many people** choose remote work so they can have lunch with their family at home.

## ✏️ Essay 7 - Who Pays for Elderly Care?

### 💡 Argument 1: Money/Economy (Government/Tax)
**Topic:** Government should pay because people paid taxes.

1.  **Pros (Of Gov Payment):**
    *   It is fair for everyone, rich or poor.
    *   Old people feel their contribution to the country was valued.
2.  **Cons (Of Gov Payment):**
    *   It costs the government a huge amount of money.
    *   Taxes might have to increase for young workers.
3.  **Reasons (Why Gov?):**
    *   Citizens paid tax for 40 years; this is the "return".
    *   The government has the power to build large care centers.
4.  **Phenomena (What we see):**
    *   Free nursing homes in countries like Sweden or Canada.
    *   Old people receiving a pension check every month.
5.  **Effects (On society):**
    *   Old people do not worry about becoming homeless.
    *   Young people are not burdened with paying for parents.
6.  **Solutions (How to fund):**
    *   Set aside a specific part of tax just for elderly care.
    *   Cut spending on less important things (like statues).
7.  **Examples (Concrete instances):**
    *   **In the UK**, the NHS provides free medical care to all retired citizens.
    *   **A retired teacher** who paid tax all her life expects the government to look after her now.

### 💡 Argument 2: Money/Economy (Poverty/Cost)
**Topic:** Families are too poor to pay; government must help to avoid poverty.

1.  **Pros (Of Gov Help):**
    *   Families can use their money for their children's education.
    *   No family goes bankrupt because of grandma's hospital bill.
2.  **Cons (Of Gov Help):**
    *   Families might stop caring for their elders personally.
    *   Waitlists for government homes might be very long.
3.  **Reasons (Why too expensive?):**
    *   Medical equipment and 24-hour nurses are very costly.
    *   Average salaries have not increased as much as medical costs.
4.  **Phenomena (What we see):**
    *   Families selling their house to pay for a nursing home.
    *   Adult children working two jobs to support aging parents.
5.  **Effects (On economy):**
    *   If families pay, the middle class becomes poor.
    *   Spending power decreases, hurting the economy.
6.  **Solutions (To prevent poverty):**
    *   Provide free healthcare for anyone over 70.
    *   Give financial aid to families who look after elders at home.
7.  **Examples (Concrete instances):**
    *   **A young couple** cannot buy a house because they spend $2000 a month on their father's care.
    *   **In the USA**, medical bills for old age are the main reason for family bankruptcy.

## ✏️ Essay 8 - Childhood Obesity

### 💡 Argument 1: Health/Well-being (Body/Food)
**Topic:** Bad food (sugar/fat) causes obesity.

1.  **Pros (Of Fast Food - reason for popularity):**
    *   It is very tasty and children love it.
    *   It is cheap and convenient for busy parents.
2.  **Cons (Of Fast Food):**
    *   It has too many calories and no vitamins.
    *   It is addictive; kids want more sugar.
3.  **Reasons (Why fat happens?):**
    *   The body stores extra sugar as fat.
    *   Processed food contains chemicals that make you hungry.
4.  **Phenomena (What we see):**
    *   Fast food restaurants on every street corner.
    *   School canteens selling pizza and chips daily.
5.  **Effects (On health):**
    *   Diabetes is becoming common in young children.
    *   Obese children get bullied and feel sad.
6.  **Solutions (How to fix):**
    *   Ban advertisements for junk food during children's TV shows.
    *   Put high taxes on sugary drinks (Sugar Tax).
7.  **Examples (Concrete instances):**
    *   **McDonald's Happy Meals** attract children with free toys, making them want to eat burgers.
    *   **A bottle of Coke** contains 10 spoons of sugar, which is way too much for a child.

### 💡 Argument 2: Society/Relationship (Lifestyle/Education)
**Topic:** Sedentary lifestyle and lack of education causes obesity.

1.  **Pros (Of Video Games - reason for lifestyle):**
    *   They are fun and keep children quiet at home.
    *   Children can play with friends online.
2.  **Cons (Of Sedentary Life):**
    *   Muscles become weak from sitting all day.
    *   Calories are not burned off.
3.  **Reasons (Why lazy?):**
    *   Parks are sometimes not safe or too far away.
    *   Screens (phones/TV) are more interesting than running.
4.  **Phenomena (What we see):**
    *   Children staring at iPads for 5 hours a day.
    *   Empty playgrounds on weekends.
5.  **Effects (On future):**
    *   A generation of unhealthy adults with heart problems.
    *   Higher healthcare costs for the country.
6.  **Solutions (How to fix):**
    *   Schools must have mandatory sports classes every day.
    *   Families should go for walks together after dinner used to exercise.
7.  **Examples (Concrete instances):**
    *   **In the past**, children played football in the street; **now** they play FIFA on Playstation.
    *   **Schools in Japan** require students to join a sports club, and obesity rates there are low.
