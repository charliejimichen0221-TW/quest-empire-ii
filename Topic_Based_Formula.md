## Topic 1：Art vs Science — 藝術不如科學重要？

**論點：** Art and science are both essential — art nourishes the mind and culture, while science saves lives. Neither should be sacrificed for the other.

**✅ Pros：**
1. Art makes **stressed people's** life **more peaceful** because they can escape daily pressure through music or painting.
   - 📌 Example: **A doctor** who paints on weekends can relax without worrying about work stress.
2. Art **preserves cultural identity** and tells the story of a nation's history, giving people a sense of **pride and belonging**.
   - 📌 Example: **The Eiffel Tower** attracts millions of tourists every year, proving art has enormous economic and cultural value.

**❌ Cons：**
1. Science makes **patients' life longer** because they can receive vaccines and surgery, leading to **real, measurable survival**.
   - 📌 Example: **Vaccines** have eradicated smallpox and saved billions of lives — art cannot do this.
2. It is too **impractical** for governments to **fund art over hospitals**, resulting in **preventable deaths**.
   - 📌 Example: **In developing countries**, every dollar spent on a gallery is a dollar taken from a hospital that could save a child.

**👁️ Phenomena：**
1. We often see **children visiting museums on weekends** but also **patients waiting hours in understaffed hospitals**.
2. Headlines show **governments cutting arts budgets first** during economic crises, reflecting society's view that science is more urgent.

**⏩ Effects：**
1. If art education is maintained, people will become **more creative and emotionally balanced**, leading to innovation in all fields.
2. Without art, we face **cultural emptiness** — a society that survives physically but feels spiritually dead.

**🔧 Solutions：**
1. The government must **use lottery money for arts and tax money for hospitals**, so both culture and health are protected.
2. Schools should **integrate art into STEM education (STEAM)**, ensuring students develop both logic and creativity.

## Topic 2：International Art — 國際藝術是否重要？

**論點：** International art is essential because it builds cross-cultural understanding and empathy in an increasingly connected world.

**✅ Pros：**
1. It makes **readers' and viewers' lives more enriched** because they can understand different cultures through foreign literature, film, and music.
   - 📌 Example: **Japanese anime** has taught millions of Western children about Japanese values like perseverance and honour.
2. It **builds cross-cultural understanding**, giving people a sense of **empathy for people from different backgrounds**.
   - 📌 Example: **The novel "To Kill a Mockingbird"** helped generations understand racial injustice in America.

**❌ Cons：**
1. It damages **local cultural identity** because **young people prefer foreign art over their own traditions**, leading to cultural erasure.
   - 📌 Example: **Korean pop music (K-pop)** has made many Asian teenagers abandon their own traditional music.
2. It is too **expensive** for **small countries** to compete with Hollywood and Western media, resulting in **local artists being ignored**.
   - 📌 Example: **Nigerian filmmakers** struggle to get funding because audiences prefer American blockbusters.

**👁️ Phenomena：**
1. We often see **teenagers worldwide listening to the same Western pop songs** while local folk music halls sit empty.
2. Statistics show **streaming platforms are dominated by English-language content**, making it harder for non-English art to survive.

**⏩ Effects：**
1. If international art exchange continues, societies will become **more tolerant and open-minded**, reducing prejudice.
2. Without protecting local art, we face **a world where every country consumes the same content**, losing unique cultural voices.

**🔧 Solutions：**
1. The government must **fund translation programs and international arts festivals** to promote cross-cultural exchange.
2. Schools should **include foreign literature and art in the curriculum**, so children appreciate diverse perspectives from a young age.

## Topic 3：Why Do People Support Arts? — 為什麼人們支持藝術？

**論點：** People support the arts because of a combination of innate human creativity and government investment in cultural infrastructure.

**✅ Pros：**
1. Art makes **communities' life richer** because festivals and galleries give them **shared experiences and social bonding**.
   - 📌 Example: **Edinburgh Festival** attracts 3 million visitors annually, uniting people through shared cultural celebration.
2. Art **attracts millions of tourists** who spend money in the city, which means **local businesses and hotels profit**.
   - 📌 Example: **The Louvre in Paris** generates billions in tourism revenue, funding local jobs and infrastructure.

**❌ Cons：**
1. It damages **taxpayers' finances** because **funding art with public money means less for healthcare and education**.
   - 📌 Example: **A small town** that spent millions on a new concert hall while its hospital needed repairs.
2. It is too **elitist** for **working-class families** who cannot afford theatre tickets or gallery memberships.
   - 📌 Example: **Opera tickets in London** cost over £100, making high culture inaccessible to ordinary people.

**👁️ Phenomena：**
1. We often see **long queues outside free museum days** but **empty seats at expensive theatres**, showing the price barrier.
2. We often see **street artists performing for tips** while **government-funded galleries display art that few people understand**.

**⏩ Effects：**
1. If arts funding continues, cities will become **more attractive and vibrant**, boosting tourism and quality of life.
2. Without arts funding, we face **a generation that cannot express emotions or appreciate beauty**, leading to a less compassionate society.

**🔧 Solutions：**
1. The government must **provide grants for young artists** and **fund public art competitions** to make art accessible to all.
2. Schools must **include art, music, and drama in the curriculum** so children from all backgrounds experience creativity.

## Topic 4：Government Fund Art vs Healthcare — 政府應資助藝術還是醫療？

**論點：** Both art and healthcare deserve funding — the question is not which one matters, but how to fund both intelligently.

**✅ Pros：**
1. Funding art makes **society's mental health better** because creative activities reduce stress, anxiety, and loneliness.
   - 📌 Example: **Art therapy programs** in UK hospitals have been shown to speed up patient recovery by 30%.
2. Art **attracts tourism and boosts the economy**, which means **the government earns money it can reinvest in healthcare**.
   - 📌 Example: **Broadway in New York** generates $15 billion annually for the city's economy.

**❌ Cons：**
1. It damages **public health** when money goes to galleries instead of hospitals, leading to **longer waiting lists**.
   - 📌 Example: **NHS waiting times in the UK** have reached record highs, while arts funding remains protected.
2. It is too **irresponsible** for **politicians** to prioritise art when **people are dying from preventable diseases**.
   - 📌 Example: **In rural Africa**, communities lack basic medicine while capital cities build modern art museums.

**👁️ Phenomena：**
1. We often see **politicians posing at gallery openings** while **nurses protest over understaffing and low pay**.
2. Headlines show **arts budgets growing** in wealthy nations while **healthcare systems collapse under demand**.

**⏩ Effects：**
1. If both are funded, society becomes **physically healthy and culturally rich**, creating a better quality of life.
2. Without art funding, we face **a society that is physically healthy but emotionally empty** — surviving but not truly living.

**🔧 Solutions：**
1. The government should **use lottery revenue for arts** and **tax revenue for healthcare**, ensuring both are funded without competing.
2. Rather than choosing one, a balanced approach would be to **integrate art into healthcare** (e.g., art therapy in hospitals).

## Topic 5：Traditional Arts Should Be Preserved — 傳統藝術應該保存？

**論點：** Traditional arts must be preserved because they carry irreplaceable cultural wisdom, but they also need to evolve to stay relevant.

**✅ Pros：**
1. It **strengthens the connection between generations**, giving young people a sense of **heritage and roots**.
   - 📌 Example: **A grandmother in Japan** teaching her granddaughter ikebana (flower arranging) keeps the tradition alive for another generation.
2. It **attracts cultural tourists** who spend money in local communities, which means **economic growth for rural areas**.
   - 📌 Example: **Flamenco dancing in Spain** draws millions of tourists every year, boosting the local economy.

**❌ Cons：**
1. It is too **expensive** for small communities because maintenance of old theatres and workshops costs thousands.
   - 📌 Example: **Traditional pottery workshops in rural China** are closing because young people move to cities for higher-paying factory jobs.
2. Without updating, traditional art might feel **boring to young people**, leading to even faster decline.
   - 📌 Example: **Classical music concerts** often have audiences with an average age over 60, showing failure to attract youth.

**👁️ Phenomena：**
1. We often see **empty folk music halls** while **K-pop concerts sell out in minutes**.
2. We often see **elderly craftsmen working alone** with no apprentices, knowing their skills will die with them.

**⏩ Effects：**
1. If preservation succeeds, communities will keep their **unique identity and attract heritage tourism**, creating sustainable income.
2. Without preservation, we face **loss of cultural identity** where every country looks and sounds the same.

**🔧 Solutions：**
1. The government must **list traditional arts as national heritage** and **fund local festivals and workshops**.
2. Schools should **include folk music and local crafts in the curriculum**, and use **social media to make traditions trendy**.

## Topic 6：Art Censorship — 藝術應該被審查？

**論點：** Art should not be fully censored, but reasonable age-based restrictions protect children while preserving artistic freedom for adults.

**✅ Pros：**
1. Censorship makes **parents' life less worrying** because they can trust that public art is safe for children.
   - 📌 Example: **A mother** can take her child to a gallery without worrying about seeing violent or sexual images.
2. It **protects vulnerable groups** from art that promotes hatred, discrimination, or dangerous behaviour.
   - 📌 Example: **Germany bans Nazi symbols** in art and media to prevent the normalisation of fascism.

**❌ Cons：**
1. It restricts **artists' creative expression** because the government decides what is "acceptable", violating artistic freedom.
   - 📌 Example: **During the Soviet era**, artists who painted political themes were imprisoned, proving censorship kills creativity.
2. It damages **society's critical thinking** because people never encounter challenging or uncomfortable ideas.
   - 📌 Example: **Banning books like "1984"** prevents citizens from questioning authority — exactly what authoritarian regimes want.

**👁️ Phenomena：**
1. We often see **controversial artworks banned from galleries** while **violent video games are sold freely** — an inconsistency.
2. We often see **artists self-censoring** to avoid controversy, producing only "safe" work that inspires no one.

**⏩ Effects：**
1. If censorship is balanced, society will be **both safe and creatively vibrant**, protecting children without silencing artists.
2. Without artistic freedom, we face **a bland, uniform culture where no one dares to challenge the status quo**.

**🔧 Solutions：**
1. The government should **use age ratings and content warnings instead of outright banning**, so adults can choose freely while children are protected.
2. Schools should **teach media literacy** so that children learn to analyse art critically rather than needing to be shielded from it.

## Topic 7：Social Media Changing Art — 社交媒體改變藝術？

**論點：** Social media has democratised art by giving everyone a platform, but it has also made art more shallow and commercialised.

**✅ Pros：**
1. It makes **unknown artists' life easier** because they can share their work with millions without needing a gallery or agent.
   - 📌 Example: **A street artist in Brazil** gained 2 million Instagram followers and now sells paintings worldwide.
2. It **connects artists across borders**, creating new styles that blend different cultural traditions.
   - 📌 Example: **TikTok music collaborations** between African and European musicians have created entirely new genres.

**❌ Cons：**
1. It damages **artists' originality** because they create content only for likes and followers, leading to shallow, commercial art.
   - 📌 Example: **Many Instagram artists** copy trending styles rather than developing their own voice, because unique art gets fewer likes.
2. It damages **attention spans** because people scroll past a masterpiece in 2 seconds, reducing art to disposable content.
   - 📌 Example: **The Mona Lisa** gets an average viewing time of 15 seconds in the Louvre — online, it gets less than 1 second.

**👁️ Phenomena：**
1. We often see **artists spending more time on captions and hashtags** than on their actual artwork.
2. We often see **AI-generated art going viral** while human artists struggle to get noticed.

**⏩ Effects：**
1. If social media art grows, **more people from disadvantaged backgrounds** will access creative careers, democratising the art world.
2. Without quality standards, we face **an ocean of mediocre content** where true artistic genius is drowned out by viral trends.

**🔧 Solutions：**
1. Rather than rejecting social media, artists should **use it as a tool to promote traditional arts** to young audiences.
2. Schools should **teach digital art alongside traditional techniques**, so students appreciate craftsmanship, not just clicks.

## Topic 8：Art as a Healthy Hobby — 藝術作為健康嗜好？

**論點：** Artistic hobbies like painting, music, and dance are essential for mental health because they provide emotional release and a sense of purpose.

**✅ Pros：**
1. It makes **stressed workers' life more relaxed** because they can express emotions through painting, music, or dance.
   - 📌 Example: **A nurse** who plays guitar after a 12-hour shift says it is the only thing that helps her forget the stress.
2. It **strengthens emotional well-being and creativity**, giving people a sense of **purpose and inner peace**.
   - 📌 Example: **A retired engineer** who took up watercolour painting said it gave him a reason to get up every morning.

**❌ Cons：**
1. It damages **productivity** if people spend too much time on hobbies instead of work or family responsibilities.
   - 📌 Example: **A father** who plays in a band every evening misses his children's bedtime routines.
2. It is too **expensive** for low-income families because instruments, art supplies, and classes cost hundreds.
   - 📌 Example: **Piano lessons** in London cost £40 per hour — unaffordable for families on minimum wage.

**👁️ Phenomena：**
1. We often see **adult colouring books and paint-by-numbers kits** selling millions, showing demand for creative stress relief.
2. Statistics show **enrolment in community art classes surged 40% after the pandemic** as people sought mental health outlets.

**⏩ Effects：**
1. If more adults pursue creative hobbies, society will see **lower rates of depression and burnout**, reducing demand on mental health services.
2. Without creative outlets, we face **a population that works and sleeps but never truly relaxes**, leading to a mental health epidemic.

**🔧 Solutions：**
1. The government should **fund free community art workshops** in every neighbourhood.
2. Schools must **include art, music, and drama in the curriculum** so children discover creative outlets early in life.

## Topic 9：Encourage Children to Read More — 鼓勵孩子多閱讀？

**論點：** Reading is essential for children's intellectual and emotional development, but it must compete with screens for their attention.

**✅ Pros：**
1. It makes **children's life more imaginative** because they can build vocabulary and discover new worlds through stories.
   - 📌 Example: **A child who reads Harry Potter** learns about loyalty, courage, and sacrifice — lessons no video game teaches.
2. It **builds empathy and critical thinking** by exposing readers to different perspectives, giving them **wisdom and open-mindedness**.
   - 📌 Example: **Studies show** children who read fiction score higher on empathy tests than those who only watch videos.

**❌ Cons：**
1. It damages **children's interest** when parents force reading as a chore, leading to a **lifelong hatred of books**.
   - 📌 Example: **A child forced to read "War and Peace" at age 10** will associate reading with boredom forever.
2. It is too **old-fashioned** to insist only on paper books when **audiobooks and e-readers can deliver the same content more accessibly**.
   - 📌 Example: **Dyslexic children** who struggle with printed text can thrive with audiobooks, proving format matters less than content.

**👁️ Phenomena：**
1. We often see **children glued to tablets watching YouTube** while **bookshelves at home collect dust**.
2. Headlines show **library visits declining by 30%** among under-16s over the past decade.

**⏩ Effects：**
1. If reading is encouraged, children will develop **stronger language skills, bigger imaginations, and better academic results**.
2. Without reading, we face **a generation with shrinking vocabularies and poor attention spans**, unable to process complex ideas.

**🔧 Solutions：**
1. Parents should **read bedtime stories every night** to build a reading habit from infancy.
2. Schools should **have a "Silent Reading Hour" every day** and stock libraries with books children actually want to read.

## Topic 10：E-books Advantages & Disadvantages — 電子書優缺點？

**論點：** E-books offer unmatched convenience and affordability, but they also come with health risks and cultural costs.

**✅ Pros：**
1. It makes **commuters' life more convenient** because they can carry thousands of books on a single device.
   - 📌 Example: **A businessman on a Tokyo train** can switch between a novel and a work report on the same Kindle.
2. It **saves money on printing and shipping**, which means publishers can offer lower prices and authors reach global markets.
   - 📌 Example: **Self-published authors on Amazon** earn 70% royalties on e-books vs 10% on printed books.

**❌ Cons：**
1. It damages **children's eyesight** because staring at screens for hours causes eye strain, leading to short-sightedness.
   - 📌 Example: **In South Korea**, 80% of teenagers are short-sighted, partly due to excessive screen reading.
2. It damages **local bookshops** because when everyone downloads books online, physical stores lose customers and close.
   - 📌 Example: **Borders bookstore chain** went bankrupt because it could not compete with Amazon's e-book prices.

**👁️ Phenomena：**
1. We often see **people reading on phones and tablets in cafés** but rarely see anyone holding a physical book.
2. We often see **"closing down" signs on independent bookshops** as online retailers dominate the market.

**⏩ Effects：**
1. If e-books grow, **more people worldwide** will access literature cheaply, increasing global literacy.
2. Without protecting physical books, we face **the death of bookshops**, which are important community gathering spaces.

**🔧 Solutions：**
1. Rather than choosing one format, a balanced approach would be to **enjoy e-books for convenience while supporting local bookshops for community**.
2. Parents should **limit children's screen reading time** and encourage physical books before bedtime to protect eyesight and sleep.

## Topic 11：Stop Supporting Public Libraries — 不再支持公共圖書館？

**論點：** Public libraries remain essential community resources that provide far more than just books — they are havens of equality and learning.

**✅ Pros：**
1. Closing libraries **saves the government money** on rent, staff, and maintenance, freeing funds for other services.
   - 📌 Example: **A single city library** costs £500,000 per year to run — money that could fund 20 school nurses.
2. Modern alternatives like **e-books, online databases, and free MOOCs** provide the same knowledge without physical buildings.
   - 📌 Example: **Khan Academy and Coursera** offer free education to anyone with internet, replacing some library functions.

**❌ Cons：**
1. It damages **poor children's education** because libraries are the only place where they can access books, computers, and quiet study space for free.
   - 📌 Example: **A child from a crowded flat** uses the library as her only quiet study space — without it, she has nowhere to do homework.
2. It destroys **community bonds** because libraries serve as meeting places, job centres, and social hubs — especially for the elderly and lonely.
   - 📌 Example: **A retired widower** visits the library every day not for books, but for human contact — it is his only social life.

**👁️ Phenomena：**
1. We often see **libraries transforming into community hubs** with coding workshops, toddler groups, and free internet access.
2. Headlines show **library closures correlating with rising youth crime** in deprived neighbourhoods.

**⏩ Effects：**
1. If libraries are modernised, they will become **even more valuable** as digital inclusion centres, job support hubs, and community spaces.
2. Without libraries, we face **a generation of poor children who never touch a book**, widening the education gap.

**🔧 Solutions：**
1. The government should **modernise libraries with digital resources, Wi-Fi, and community programs** rather than closing them.
2. Libraries should **partner with schools and local businesses** to offer after-school tutoring, job workshops, and cultural events.

## Topic 12：Work for Yourself vs a Company — 自僱 vs 打工？

**論點：** Being self-employed offers freedom and fulfilment, but comes with financial instability and immense pressure.

**✅ Pros：**
1. It makes **entrepreneurs' life more fulfilling** because they can set their own hours and follow their passion.
   - 📌 Example: **A freelance graphic designer** chooses to work from Bali, combining travel with income on her own terms.
2. It **builds self-confidence and independence**, giving entrepreneurs a sense of **personal achievement and control over their destiny**.
   - 📌 Example: **Steve Jobs** started Apple in a garage because he refused to follow someone else's vision.

**❌ Cons：**
1. It damages **entrepreneurs' mental health** because the constant risk of failure and financial pressure never stops, leading to burnout.
   - 📌 Example: **A café owner** works 16-hour days, hasn't taken a holiday in 3 years, and still barely covers rent.
2. It is too **risky** for self-employed people because there is no guaranteed salary or sick leave, resulting in financial instability.
   - 📌 Example: **During COVID**, freelancers received no furlough pay while employed workers were protected by government schemes.

**👁️ Phenomena：**
1. We often see **"Be Your Own Boss" ads on social media** promising freedom but hiding the reality of 80-hour weeks.
2. Statistics show **60% of small businesses fail within 5 years**, proving that passion alone does not guarantee success.

**⏩ Effects：**
1. If entrepreneurship is supported, more people will **create innovative products and services**, driving economic growth.
2. Without safety nets, we face **a generation of burned-out freelancers** with no pension, no savings, and deteriorating mental health.

**🔧 Solutions：**
1. The government should **provide startup grants, tax breaks, and free business mentoring** for new entrepreneurs.
2. Rather than choosing one extreme, a balanced approach would be to **start a side business while still employed**, ensuring income security while testing your idea.

## Topic 13：Spending vs Saving Money — 花錢 vs 存錢？

**論點：** A balance between spending and saving is essential — enjoying life today while building security for tomorrow.

**✅ Pros：**
1. Spending stimulates the economy because **consumers buying goods creates jobs and grows businesses**.
   - 📌 Example: **During Black Friday sales**, retailers hire millions of temporary workers, boosting seasonal employment.
2. Saving protects families from disaster because **an emergency fund prevents one job loss from becoming homelessness**.
   - 📌 Example: **A family with 6 months' savings** can survive a redundancy without borrowing or losing their home.

**❌ Cons：**
1. It damages **people's relationships** because constant money worries create arguments between partners, leading to divorce.
   - 📌 Example: **Studies show money problems** are the number one cause of divorce in the UK and USA.
2. It is too **easy to overspend** because credit cards and "buy now, pay later" apps make borrowing effortless.
   - 📌 Example: **The average UK adult** has £3,500 in credit card debt, much of it from impulse purchases.

**👁️ Phenomena：**
1. We often see **people lining up for hours to buy the newest iPhone** even when their current phone works perfectly.
2. We often see **"buy now, pay later" apps** used by teenagers who do not understand they are borrowing real money.

**⏩ Effects：**
1. If financial literacy improves, society will have **fewer bankruptcies and financial crises**, creating more stable communities.
2. Without financial education, we face **serious debt epidemics and mental health crises** from constant financial anxiety.

**🔧 Solutions：**
1. Schools must make **"Money Management" a compulsory subject** from age 14, teaching budgeting, saving, and avoiding debt traps.
2. Rather than forcing people to save everything, a balanced approach would be to **teach budgeting and set up auto-savings programs**, so people enjoy life while building security.

## Topic 14：Small Local Shops vs Chain Stores — 小店 vs 大店？

**論點：** Local shops preserve community character and diversity, but they need government protection to survive against chain store pricing power.

**✅ Pros：**
1. Local shops **keep money circulating in the community**, which means the neighbourhood stays vibrant and diverse.
   - 📌 Example: **A family-owned bakery** sources flour from a local farm and employs teenagers from the neighbourhood.
2. They **preserve neighbourhood character and personal relationships**, giving customers a sense of community and trust.
   - 📌 Example: **A local butcher** who remembers every customer's name provides a personal experience no supermarket can match.

**❌ Cons：**
1. It is too **unfair** for small shop owners because chain stores can offer lower prices through mass production, resulting in local shops closing.
   - 📌 Example: **When Walmart enters a small town**, on average 4 local shops close within the first year.
2. It damages **consumers' budgets** when they cannot access cheaper chain-store prices, especially during a cost-of-living crisis.
   - 📌 Example: **A single mother** cannot afford to buy organic bread from the bakery when a supermarket loaf costs half the price.

**👁️ Phenomena：**
1. We often see **"closing down" signs on small family bakeries** while a Starbucks opens across the street.
2. We often see **identical high streets in every city** — the same chains, the same logos, the same experience everywhere.

**⏩ Effects：**
1. If local shops thrive, cities will maintain **unique character, diverse choices, and strong neighbourhoods**.
2. Without protecting small businesses, we face **identical high streets in every city**, destroying local culture and diversity.

**🔧 Solutions：**
1. The government must **reduce tax for small businesses** and **limit chain store expansion in residential areas**.
2. Rather than blocking chain stores entirely, a balanced approach would be to **limit their numbers per area and give rent subsidies to local shops**.

## Topic 15：Buy Domestic vs Foreign Products — 買國貨 vs 外貨？

**論點：** Supporting domestic products strengthens the local economy, but consumers should still have the freedom to choose.

**✅ Pros：**
1. Buying domestic **supports local factories and farms**, which means jobs stay in the country and the economy grows from within.
   - 📌 Example: **"Made in Germany"** branding has kept German manufacturing strong, employing millions.
2. It **reduces carbon emissions** from international shipping, which means a smaller environmental footprint.
   - 📌 Example: **Buying local apples** instead of imported ones saves thousands of tons of CO₂ from air freight.

**❌ Cons：**
1. It restricts **consumers' personal choice** because the government tells people what they should buy, violating individual liberty.
   - 📌 Example: **Banning imported cheese in Canada** means consumers pay double for domestic alternatives.
2. It is too **protectionist** and can trigger trade wars, resulting in other countries taxing your exports in retaliation.
   - 📌 Example: **US-China tariff wars** raised prices for consumers in both countries without benefiting either.

**👁️ Phenomena：**
1. We often see **"Buy Local" campaigns** on social media but consumers still choosing cheaper imported goods online.
2. Statistics show **local products cost 20-40% more** than imports, making patriotic shopping a luxury for the wealthy.

**⏩ Effects：**
1. If domestic industries are supported, the country will achieve **greater economic independence and job security**.
2. Without consumer freedom, we face **higher prices and fewer choices**, punishing the poorest families the most.

**🔧 Solutions：**
1. The government should **support local brands through tax breaks** while allowing consumer choice.
2. Rather than banning foreign products, a balanced approach would be to **make domestic products more competitive through innovation and quality**.

## Topic 16：Children Should Learn Money Management — 理財教育？

**論點：** Financial literacy should be taught in schools because money skills are essential for adult life but rarely taught at home.

**✅ Pros：**
1. It makes **teenagers' future more secure** because they learn to budget, save, and avoid debt traps before entering the real world.
   - 📌 Example: **A 16-year-old who learns compound interest** will start saving early and retire comfortably.
2. It **prevents the cycle of generational debt** by giving children from poor families the knowledge their parents never had.
   - 📌 Example: **In Australia**, schools that teach financial literacy saw a 25% reduction in student loan defaults.

**❌ Cons：**
1. It is too **boring** for young children who cannot relate to mortgages and investments.
   - 📌 Example: **A 10-year-old** cares about pocket money, not pension plans — forcing adult concepts too early kills interest.
2. It takes time **away from core subjects** like maths and science, which universities and employers value more.
   - 📌 Example: **An overcrowded school timetable** cannot fit another compulsory subject without cutting something else.

**👁️ Phenomena：**
1. We often see **university students applying for credit cards without understanding interest rates**, then drowning in debt by graduation.

**⏩ Effects：**

## Topic 117：Olympics — Worth Hosting? — 奧運值得辦嗎？

**論點：** The Olympics inspire nations and unite the world through sport, but the enormous cost and corruption must be addressed.

**✅ Pros：**
1. Hosting the Olympics **boosts infrastructure** — new transport, stadiums, and housing that benefit the city for decades.
   - 📌 Example: **London 2012** regenerated East London, creating 11,000 new homes and transforming a neglected area.
2. They **inspire national pride and youth participation** in sport, improving public health.
   - 📌 Example: **After the 2012 Olympics**, youth participation in athletics in the UK rose by 18%.

**❌ Cons：**
1. It is too **expensive** — most host cities go billions over budget and are left with unused "white elephant" stadiums.
   - 📌 Example: **The 2016 Rio Olympics** cost $13 billion, and many facilities are now abandoned and crumbling.
2. It damages **local communities** when residents are displaced to build Olympic venues.
   - 📌 Example: **Beijing's 2008 Olympics** displaced 1.5 million residents, demolishing historic neighbourhoods.

**👁️ Phenomena：**
1. We often see **fewer cities bidding to host** because the costs and risks are now well-documented.
2. Statistics show **every Olympics since 1960 has gone over budget** — by an average of 172%.

**⏩ Effects：**
1. If reformed, the Olympics can **inspire, unite, and leave lasting benefits** for host cities.
2. Without cost controls, we face **bankrupt cities and abandoned stadiums** — monuments to waste, not sport.

**🔧 Solutions：**
1. The IOC must **cap budgets, require existing venue use**, and share costs across multiple host cities.
2. Rather than building from scratch, a balanced approach would be to **reuse existing facilities and invest in legacy planning** so communities benefit long after the closing ceremony.

## Topic 118：Tourism — Benefit or Curse? — 旅遊是福還是禍？

**論點：** Tourism creates jobs and cultural exchange, but overtourism destroys environments, raises living costs, and erodes local identity.

**✅ Pros：**
1. Tourism **creates jobs and economic growth** in communities that have few other industries.
   - 📌 Example: **The Maldives' economy** depends 60% on tourism — it funds schools, hospitals, and infrastructure.
2. It **promotes cultural understanding** when travellers experience different ways of life and return home with broader perspectives.
   - 📌 Example: **A student who volunteered in rural Peru** returned home with a lifelong commitment to social justice.

**❌ Cons：**
1. It damages **local environments** when mass tourism pollutes beaches, destroys coral reefs, and overwhelms infrastructure.
   - 📌 Example: **Maya Bay in Thailand** was closed for 3 years after tourism destroyed 80% of its coral reef.
2. It damages **local communities** when Airbnb drives up rents and displaces long-term residents.
   - 📌 Example: **Barcelona residents protested** against mass tourism after rents rose 50% and local shops were replaced by souvenir stores.

**👁️ Phenomena：**
1. We often see **"Instagram tourism"** where visitors flock to photogenic locations, overwhelming fragile environments.
2. Statistics show **tourism contributes 10% of global GDP** but also 8% of global carbon emissions.

**⏩ Effects：**
1. If managed sustainably, tourism will **continue creating jobs and cultural exchange** without destruction.
2. Without limits, we face **overtourism destroying the very places that attract visitors** — a self-defeating cycle.

**🔧 Solutions：**
1. The government must **limit visitor numbers at fragile sites** and invest tourism revenue in environmental protection.
2. Rather than banning tourism, a balanced approach would be to **promote sustainable tourism** — eco-lodges, community-based tourism, and off-season travel incentives.
