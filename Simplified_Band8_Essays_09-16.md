## ✏️ Essay 9 — Children and Paid Work

**題目：** In many countries, children are engaged in some kind of paid work. Some people regard this as completely wrong, while others consider it as valuable work experience. Discuss both views and give your opinion.

**選用 Ideas：**
1. **Society/Relationship (Education):** Work hurts school studies.
2. **Health/Well-being (Safety/Body):** Work makes children tired.
3. **Money/Economy (Jobs/Income):** Work gives experience for future jobs.

**Essay:**

Should children do paid work? Some people think this is wrong, while others believe it is good experience. I think that a small amount of work is good for their future jobs.

On the one hand, working can be bad for children's education and health. If a student works many hours, they might not have enough time to do their homework. This can lead to low grades in school. Also, working can make them too tired. **For example, a student who works late in a shop on Sunday might fall asleep in class on Monday morning.** This is not good for their body or their brain.

On the other hand, working gives valuable experience. When young people have a job, they learn how to be responsible and punctual. **For instance, a teenager working in a busy restaurant learns to be polite to customers and manage their time well.** These skills are very useful for their future careers. It helps them understand the business world and prepares them to earn a good income as adults.

In conclusion, although working too much can hurt education and health, I believe a little work experience is beneficial. It teaches important skills that help children succeed in their future jobs.

## ✏️ Essay 10 — Air Transport of Food

**題目：** Air transport is increasingly used to export fruit and vegetables to countries where they cannot be grown or are out of season. What are the advantages and disadvantages of this?

**選用 Ideas：**
1. **Health/Well-being (Body/Nutrition):** Fresh food is good for health.
2. **Society/Relationship (Environment):** Planes cause pollution.

**Essay:**

Nowadays, airplanes carry fruit and vegetables to many different countries. This has both advantages and disadvantages.

The main advantage is better health for people. In some countries, it is too cold or dry to grow fresh fruit. If planes import these foods, people can eat a variety of vitamins and minerals all year round. **For example, people in cold countries like Norway can eat fresh bananas and mangoes even in the middle of winter.** This healthy diet improves the body and prevents sickness.

However, the disadvantage is the bad effect on the environment. Airplanes use a lot of fuel and release gases into the air. This pollution contributes to global warming. **For instance, flying vegetables from Africa to Europe creates much more carbon dioxide than growing them on a local farm.** This dirty air is a big problem for our society and can hurt people's health in the long run.

In conclusion, importing food by air is good for our nutrition and body, but it is bad for the environment because of pollution. We need to balance these two things.

## ✏️ Essay 11 — Debt from Unnecessary Purchases

**題目：** Some people get into debt by buying things they do not need and cannot afford. What are the reasons for this behaviour? What action can be taken to prevent it?

**選用 Ideas：**
1. **Health/Well-being (Happiness/Mind):** Buying things makes people feel happy for a short time.
2. **Society/Relationship (Culture/Ads):** Ads make people want things.
3. **Society/Relationship (Education):** Teach money management.

**Essay:**

Many people today have debt because they buy things they do not need. This essay will look at the reasons for this and suggest some solutions.

One reason is that shopping creates temporary happiness, driven by advertising. When people feel stressed or sad, they might buy new clothes or gadgets to feel better. Ads make this worse by showing beautiful lives with expensive products. **For example, a person might use a credit card to buy a luxury bag they cannot afford just because a famous star has one.** This makes them feel happy for a moment, but the debt remains.

To stop this, education is the best solution. Schools should teach students how to manage money and the danger of debt. **For instance, math classes can teach children how much extra money they must pay if they borrow from a bank.** If young people understand the high cost of interest, they will be more careful with their spending.

In conclusion, people buy unnecessary things because of emotional needs and advertising. However, better financial education can help solve this problem.

## ✏️ Essay 12 — Resistance to Change

**題目：** People naturally resist making changes in their lives. What kinds of problems can this cause? What solutions can you suggest?

**選用 Ideas：**
1. **Health/Well-being (Mind/Fear):** Fear of change causes stress.
2. **Money/Economy (Jobs/Career):** Staying in one job stops career growth.
3. **Society/Relationship (Friends/Family):** Support from others helps.

**Essay:**

It is common for people to dislike change. They prefer to stay in their comfort zone. This resistance can cause problems, but there are solutions.

The main problem is that avoiding change stops personal growth and can hurt a career. A person might stay in a boring job because they are afraid to look for a new one. **For example, an employee might stay in a low-paying position for ten years simply because they fear the interview process.** This fear stops them from getting a better salary. Also, staying in a bad situation can cause unhappiness and stress, which is not good for the mind.

To solve this, people need to change their thinking and get support. They should look at change as an opportunity, not a danger. Support from friends and family is distinctively helpful. **For instance, talking to a successful friend about their new job can give a person the confidence to make a change.** This encouragement makes it easier to take the first step towards a new life.

In conclusion, resisting change can hurt a person's career and happiness. However, positive thinking and support from friends can help people accept new challenges.

## ✏️ Essay 13 — Museum Admission Fees

**題目：** Many museums charge for admission, while others are free. Do you think the advantages of charging people for admission to museums outweigh the disadvantages?

**選用 Ideas：**
1. **Money/Economy (Income/Business):** Museums need money to survive.
2. **Society/Relationship (Equality/Education):** Free museums are good for poor people.
3. **Opinion:** Charging is better (or necessary).

**Essay:**

Some museums sell tickets, while others are free. I think the advantages of charging money are greater than the disadvantages, because museums need income to stay open.

The main disadvantage of charging is that poor people might not be able to visit. Museums are important for education and culture. If the ticket is expensive, families with low income cannot enter. **For example, a family with three children might decide not to visit a science museum if the total cost is over $50.** This is not fair for their children, who lose the chance to learn history and art.

However, the advantage is that ticket money keeps the museum alive. Running a museum costs a lot of money. They need to pay for electricity, staff, and for cleaning ancient objects. **For instance, the money from tickets can be used to repair broken air conditioning to protect old paintings from heat and humidity.** Without this income, the museum might close down, and nobody could visit.

In conclusion, although free entry is good for equality, I believe charging fees is necessary. It ensures the museum has enough money to protect our history for the future.

## ✏️ Essay 14 — Job Satisfaction

**題目：** What are the factors that contribute to job satisfaction? How realistic is the expectation of job satisfaction for all workers?

**選用 Ideas：**
1. **Money/Economy (Income):** Good pay makes people satisfied.
2. **Health/Well-being (Happiness/Respect):** Feeling respected reduces stress.
3. **Society/Relationship (Poverty):** Not everyone can be picky.

**Essay:**

Most adults spend a lot of time at work. Job satisfaction is important for their lives. I think the main factors are money and respect, but sadly, not everyone can have a perfect job.

The first factor is money and respect. Employees need a fair salary to pay for their life needs. If the income is good, they feel their work is valuable. Also, the work environment is key. **For example, if a manager listens to the workers' ideas and says "thank you", the staff will feel respected and happy.** This positive feeling reduces stress and is good for mental well-being.

However, expecting every worker to be satisfied is not realistic. In the real world, many people have to take any job just to survive. **For instance, a factory worker may do a repetitive and boring task for 10 hours a day just to pay for food and rent.** They do this because they need the money to feed their family, not because they love the work.

In conclusion, good pay and respect bring job satisfaction. But for many poor people in society, finding a perfect job is impossible, and they must work just to live.

## ✏️ Essay 15 — Social Status vs Old-Fashioned Values

**題目：** A person's worth nowadays seems to be judged according to social status and material possessions. Old-fashioned values such as honour, kindness, and trust no longer seem important. To what extent do you agree or disagree?

**選用 Ideas：**
1. **Money/Economy (Wealth):** People admire rich people.
2. **Society/Relationship (Community/Kindness):** Kindness is forgotten but essential.

**Essay:**

In modern society, many people judge others by their money and status. I agree with this view. I think we care too much about material things and have forgotten old values like kindness.

Today, people often look at what car a person drives or what clothes they wear. If someone is rich and successful in business, society respects them. **For example, people often admire a businessman who owns a luxury yacht more than a nurse who saves lives but has little money.** This focus on money creates a culture where appearances are more important than character. It makes people stressed because they always want to buy more things.

In contrast, old values like honour and kindness are seen as less important. In the past, helping neighbors and being honest was the most important thing in a community. **For instance, simply helping an old lady carry her heavy shopping bags is an act of kindness that is often forgotten today.** This is a sad change because kindness is what makes a society strong and happy.

In conclusion, I agree that money and status are now more important than values. We need to remember that kindness and trust are the real qualities that make a person good.

## ✏️ Essay 16 — Internet Problems and Solutions

**題目：** What are the most serious problems associated with the internet and what are the possible solutions?

**選用 Ideas：**
1. **Society/Relationship (Crime):** Theft and scams.
2. **Health/Well-being (Mind/Bullying):** Cyberbullying hurts mental health.
3. **Government/Society (Laws/Education):** Rules and teaching.

**Essay:**

The internet is very useful, but it also brings serious problems. The biggest issues are crime and bullying. I believe education and strict laws can help solve these.

One major problem is online crime and bullying. Bad people can steal money or personal information using the internet. **For example, a criminal can steal credit card numbers from a shopping website and take money from a bank account.** Another problem is cyberbullying. On social media, people can say mean things to others. This hurts the victims' feelings and causes mental stress.

To solve these problems, the government and schools must act. To stop crime, the government needs stricter laws to catch online criminals. To stop bullying, education is key. **For instance, schools can have lessons to teach children how to protect their passwords and how to report mean messages.** If children understand online safety, they will be safer.

In conclusion, crime and bullying are serious internet problems hurt our money and our minds. However, with good laws and education, we can make the online world safer.
