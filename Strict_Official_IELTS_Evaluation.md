## 🧐 Detailed Criterion Analysis (Selected Examples)

### 1. The "High Achiever" Examples (Band 8.0 Range)
**Essay 01 (Smoking) & Essay 18 (Refugees)**

*   **Task Response (9.0):**
    *   *Criterion:* "Fully addresses all parts of the task. Presents a fully developed position with relevant, fully extended and well-supported ideas."
    *   *Evidence:* In Essay 18, the distinction between "moral duty" (saving lives) and "economic investment" (future taxes) is incredibly sharp. The examples given (escaping war zones, opening small businesses) perfectly extend the argument. There is zero fluff.
*   **Coherence & Cohesion (8.0):**
    *   *Criterion:* "Sequences information and ideas logically. Manages all aspects of cohesion well."
    *   *Evidence:* The progression from "First... Second... In conclusion" is standard, but the *internal* logic of the paragraphs (Idea -> Explanation -> Example) is flawless.
*   **Lexical Resource (7.0):**
    *   *Criterion:* "Uses a sufficient range of vocabulary to allow some flexibility and precision."
    *   *Constraint:* You avoided C1 words. However, context-specific words like "passive smoking", "government spending", "tax revenue", "refugees", "moral duty" ensure it reaches Band 7. It avoids Band 6 because there are no errors.
*   **Grammar (7.0 - 8.0):**
    *   *Criterion:* "Uses a variety of complex structures. Produces frequent error-free sentences."
    *   *Evidence:* The sentences are structurally sound but rely heavily on standard templates ("If... then...", "This means that..."). To get a strict 8 or 9, we would need more variety in sentence openings.

### 2. The "Solid Pass" Examples (Band 7.0 Range)
**Essay 05 (Media) & Essay 14 (Job Satisfaction)**

*   **Task Response (8.0):**
    *   Still very high. The ideas are clear. In Essay 05, the link between "violence in movies" and "crime" is a bit simplistic compared to a Band 9 argument, but it is fully supported and accepted in IELTS.
