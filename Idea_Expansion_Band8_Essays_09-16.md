## ✏️ Essay 9 - Children and Paid Work

### 💡 Argument 1: Society/Health (Education vs Work)
**Topic:** Working too much hurts education and health.

1.  **Pros (Of NOT working/Focusing on study):**
    *   Children have enough time to finish their homework.
    *   They get enough sleep and grow taller and stronger.
2.  **Cons (Of Working):**
    *   They might fall asleep during important lessons.
    *   Their grades will drop because they have no time to study.
3.  **Reasons (Why it hurts?):**
    *   A child's brain needs rest to learn new things.
    *   Physical work (like carrying boxes) is too hard for young bodies.
4.  **Phenomena (What we see):**
    *   Teenagers rushing from school to a shop job without eating.
    *   Teachers complaining that students are tired in class.
5.  **Effects (Long-term):**
    *   These children might fail exams and not go to university.
    *   They might have health problems (like back pain) early in life.
6.  **Solutions (How to fix):**
    *   Laws should stop children from working after 8 PM.
    *   Parents must limit work hours to weekends only.
7.  **Examples (Concrete instances):**
    *   **A student** named Tom worked until midnight and failed his math test.
    *   **Children in poor countries** often work in factories instead of going to school.

### 💡 Argument 2: Money/Economy (Experience)
**Topic:** Work provides valuable experience and money.

1.  **Pros (Of Working):**
    *   Children learn the value of money (it is hard to earn).
    *   They learn how to talk to strangers politely (customer service).
2.  **Cons (Of NOT Working):**
    *   Children might become spoiled and think money is free.
    *   They might be shy and afraid of the real world.
3.  **Reasons (Why it helps?):**
    *   Schools do not teach practical skills like handling cash.
    *   Having a boss teaches discipline and punctuality.
4.  **Phenomena (What we see):**
    *   A young person buying their own phone with their own salary.
    *   Students listing "part-time job" on their university application.
5.  **Effects (Future):**
    *   They will find a full-time job faster because they have a resume.
    *   They manage their own bank accounts better than others.
6.  **Solutions (How to encourage):**
    *   Schools should offer internships for older students.
    *   Companies should have special "training jobs" for youth.
7.  **Examples (Concrete instances):**
    *   **A teenager** who delivers newspapers learns to wake up early every day.
    *   **Successful businessmen** often say they started working when they were 15.

## ✏️ Essay 10 - Air Transport of Food

### 💡 Argument 1: Health/Well-being (Nutrition)
**Topic:** Importing food improves health (diet variety).

1.  **Pros (Of Imported Food):**
    *   We can eat fresh fruit like strawberries even in winter.
    *   People have more choices in the supermarket.
2.  **Cons (Of Local Food Only):**
    *   Diet might be boring (only potatoes and carrots in winter).
    *   Risk of vitamin deficiency if local crops fail.
3.  **Reasons (Why we need it?):**
    *   Some countries are too cold or dry to grow fruit.
    *   Doctors say we need 5 different vegetables a day.
4.  **Phenomena (What we see):**
    *   Bananas from Africa sold in shops in Norway.
    *   Fresh salad available all year round.
5.  **Effects (On health):**
    *   Children grow stronger because of good nutrition.
    *   Fewer people get sick from poor diet.
6.  **Solutions (To keep supply):**
    *   Governments should sign trade deals with farming countries.
    *   Better refrigerators to keep imported food fresh longer.
7.  **Examples (Concrete instances):**
    *   **Oranges** are full of Vitamin C, but they don't grow in Canada, so planes bring them.
    *   **People in desert countries** like Dubai rely on planes for almost all their vegetables.

### 💡 Argument 2: Society/Relationship (Environment)
**Topic:** Air transport causes pollution (Global Warming).

1.  **Pros (Of Local Food - Environmental view):**
    *   It travels a short distance, so very little pollution.
    *   It supports local farmers in our own country.
2.  **Cons (Of Air Transport):**
    *   Planes burn a lot of fuel (kerosene).
    *   It creates a huge "carbon footprint" for a simple apple.
3.  **Reasons (Why pollution?):**
    *   Airplanes release CO2 directly into the high atmosphere.
    *   Packaging (plastic) is needed to protect food on planes.
4.  **Phenomena (What we see):**
    *   Black smoke trails in the sky from confused air traffic.
    *   Statistics showing rising global temperatures.
5.  **Effects (On planet):**
    *   Climate change gets worse (more storms, hot weather).
    *   Ice melts and sea levels rise.
6.  **Solutions (How to fix):**
    *   Buy "local and seasonal" food whenever possible.
    *   Add a heavy tax on food that comes by plane.
7.  **Examples (Concrete instances):**
    *   **Asparagus** flown from Peru to London creates more pollution than driving a car for an hour.
    *   **Consumers** choosing local apples instead of imported mangoes helps the planet.

## ✏️ Essay 11 - Debt from Unnecessary Purchases

### 💡 Argument 1: Health/Psychology (Happiness & Ads)
**Topic:** Buying things for temporary happiness (Retail Therapy).

1.  **Pros (Of Buying - short term):**
    *   You feel excited when you get a new toy or phone.
    *   It distracts you from a bad day at work.
2.  **Cons (Of Emotional Buying):**
    *   The happiness leaves quickly, but the bill remains.
    *   You fill your house with junk you do not use.
3.  **Reasons (Why we do it?):**
    *   Advertisements promise that products make us "cool".
    *   Social media makes us jealous of other people's lives.
4.  **Phenomena (What we see):**
    *   People lining up for hours to buy the newest iPhone.
    *   Wardrobes full of clothes that still have price tags on them.
5.  **Effects (On life):**
    *   Serious debt and stress about money.
    *   Mental health problems from constant "wanting".
6.  **Solutions (How to stop):**
    *   Wait 24 hours before buying anything expensive.
    *   Unfollow "influencers" who try to sell products online.
7.  **Examples (Concrete instances):**
    *   **A girl** buys a $2000 handbag to look like her favorite singer, even if she is poor.
    *   **Many people** buy expensive gym equipment in January but never use it.

### 💡 Argument 2: Society/Education (Financial Literacy)
**Topic:** Education is the solution to debt.

1.  **Pros (Of Financial Education):**
    *   Young people understand credit cards and interest rates.
    *   They learn to save money for emergencies.
2.  **Cons (Of No Education):**
    *   People sign contracts they do not understand.
    *   They fall into traps set by banks.
3.  **Reasons (Why teach it?):**
    *   Parents often do not talk about money at home.
    *   Math in school is too theoretical, not practical.
4.  **Phenomena (What we see):**
    *   Students graduating with huge credit card debt.
    *   Classes teaching how to make a monthly budget.
5.  **Effects (On society):**
    *   A richer population with more savings.
    *   Fewer bankruptcies and financial crises.
6.  **Solutions (How to implement):**
    *   Make "Money Management" a compulsory subject in high school.
    *   Banks should be forced to explain risks simply.
7.  **Examples (Concrete instances):**
    *   **A student** who learns about interest rates will avoid paying only the "minimum payment" on credit cards.
    *   **Schools** can play monopoly-style games to teach kids about rent and taxes.

## ✏️ Essay 12 - Resistance to Change

### 💡 Argument 1: Money/Health (Fear & Career)
**Topic:** Fear of change hurts career and causes stagnancy.

1.  **Pros (Of Changing):**
    *   You learn new skills and become smarter.
    *   You can find a job that pays more money.
2.  **Cons (Of Staying/Resisting):**
    *   You become bored and unhappy with life.
    *   Your skills become old and useless.
3.  **Reasons (Why resist?):**
    *   Humans are naturally afraid of the unknown.
    *   We like comfort and routine (doing the same thing).
4.  **Phenomena (What we see):**
    *   Employees staying in a company they hate for 20 years.
    *   People refusing to learn how to use a computer.
5.  **Effects (On life):**
    *   Missed opportunities for success.
    *   Regret when you are old ("I wish I had tried...").
6.  **Solutions (How to fix):**
    *   Take small steps (change one small thing at a time).
    *   Read stories about successful people who took risks.
7.  **Examples (Concrete instances):**
    *   **Kodak** refused to change to digital cameras and went bankrupt.
    *   **A taxi driver** who learns to use Uber might earn more money than before.

### 💡 Argument 2: Society/Relationship (Support)
**Topic:** Support from friends helps overcome fear.

1.  **Pros (Of Support):**
    *   Friends can encourage you when you are scared.
    *   They can give advice if they have done it before.
2.  **Cons (Of Doing it Alone):**
    *   Fear grows bigger in your head when you are lonely.
    *   You might give up easily if things get hard.
3.  **Reasons (Why share?):**
    *   Saying your fear out loud makes it less scary.
    *   Humans are social animals who need validation.
4.  **Phenomena (What we see):**
    *   Support groups for people changing careers.
    *   Friends celebrating a new job offer together.
5.  **Effects (On success):**
    *   Higher chance of succeeding in the new change.
    *   Stronger friendships built on trust.
6.  **Solutions (How to get support):**
    *   Tell your family your plan so they can help.
    *   Find a mentor or coach to guide you.
7.  **Examples (Concrete instances):**
    *   **Someone quitting smoking** is more successful if their friends cheer for them.
    *   **Starting a business** is easier if your partner supports your idea emotionally.

## ✏️ Essay 13 - Museum Admission Fees

### 💡 Argument 1: Society/Equality (Free Entry)
**Topic:** Free museums allow poor people to learn.

1.  **Pros (Of Free Entry):**
    *   Everyone can enter, even homeless people or students.
    *   Schools can bring classes for free field trips.
2.  **Cons (Of Admission Fees):**
    *   It creates a wall between rich and poor.
    *   Culture becomes only for the wealthy.
3.  **Reasons (Why free?):**
    *   It is unfair to deny education based on money.
    *   History belongs to everyone in the country.
4.  **Phenomena (What we see):**
    *   Long lines of families visiting free museums on weekends.
    *   Tourists choosing cities with free attractions.
5.  **Effects (On society):**
    *   A more educated public.
    *   Higher appreciation for art and history.
6.  **Solutions (How to keep free):**
    *   Government uses tax money to pay for the museum.
    *   Wealthy people donate money to support it.
7.  **Examples (Concrete instances):**
    *   **The British Museum** in London is free, so millions of people visit every year.
    *   **A poor student** can study ancient history by looking at real mummies for free.

### 💡 Argument 2: Money/Business (Paid Entry)
**Topic:** Fees are necessary for museum survival.

1.  **Pros (Of Fees):**
    *   The museum has money to repair the building.
    *   They can buy new paintings or artifacts.
2.  **Cons (Of No Income):**
    *   The museum looks dirty and old.
    *   Staff are paid very low salaries.
3.  **Reasons (Why charge?):**
    *   Electricity, security, and cleaning are expensive.
    *   Government funding is often not enough.
4.  **Phenomena (What we see):**
    *   Expensive special exhibitions (like dinosaurs) that cost extra.
    *   Souvenir shops selling expensive gifts to make money.
5.  **Effects (On museum):**
    *   Better facilities (clean toilets, good lighting).
    *   The museum can stay open for many years.
6.  **Solutions (Compromise):**
    *   Charge tourists but let locals enter for free.
    *   Have one "Free Day" every month.
7.  **Examples (Concrete instances):**
    *   **The Louvre** in Paris charges money, which helps maintain the famous Mona Lisa.
    *   **Small local museums** often close down because they cannot pay the electricity bill.

## ✏️ Essay 14 - Job Satisfaction

### 💡 Argument 1: Money/Respect (Satisfaction Factors)
**Topic:** Money and respect create satisfaction.

1.  **Pros (Of Good Pay/Respect):**
    *   Employees feel valued and important.
    *   They work harder and stay loyal to the company.
2.  **Cons (Of Low Pay/Disrespect):**
    *   Staff feel angry and unmotivated.
    *   They will leave the job as soon as possible.
3.  **Reasons (Why important?):**
    *   Money solves life problems (rent, food).
    *   Respect fulfills the human need for dignity.
4.  **Phenomena (What we see):**
    *   Companies giving bonuses at the end of the year.
    *   Managers saying "Good job" for a small task.
5.  **Effects (On company):**
    *   High productivity and profit.
    *   Low staff turnover (people don't quit).
6.  **Solutions (How to improve):**
    *   Regular salary reviews to match inflation.
    *   Training for managers on how to treat staff nicely.
7.  **Examples (Concrete instances):**
    *   **A nurse** feels happy when a patient says "Thank you" for her care.
    *   **Software engineers** are satisfied because they earn a high salary for their skills.

### 💡 Argument 2: Society/Poverty (Reality)
**Topic:** Satisfaction is unrealistic for the poor; survival is the goal.

1.  **Pros (Of Any Job):**
    *   It puts food on the table for the family.
    *   It prevents homelessness.
2.  **Cons (Of Picky Attitude):**
    *   If you wait for a "perfect" job, you might starve.
    *   Refusing work can make you lazy.
3.  **Reasons (Why unrealistic?):**
    *   Unskilled workers have few choices.
    *   Competition for good jobs is very high.
4.  **Phenomena (What we see):**
    *   People cleaning streets or working in hot factories.
    *   Immigrants taking difficult jobs that locals refuse.
5.  **Effects (On life):**
    *   Life is hard and tiring, but the family survives.
    *   Hope that their children will have better jobs.
6.  **Solutions (To help them):**
    *   Government should set a Minimum Wage law.
    *   Offer free training to help them get better skills.
7.  **Examples (Concrete instances):**
    *   **A cleaner** works night shifts not because he loves it, but to buy food.
    *   **Refugees** often accept dangerous construction jobs just to have an income.

## ✏️ Essay 15 - Social Status vs Values

### 💡 Argument 1: Money/Status (Modern Values)
**Topic:** Society judges worth by money/material goods.

1.  **Pros (Of Ambition - counter-point):**
    *   It encourages people to work hard to succeed.
    *   Economic growth happens when people want to buy things.
2.  **Cons (Of Materialism):**
    *   People judge you by your shoes, not your heart.
    *   It creates a shallow and fake society.
3.  **Reasons (Why this happens?):**
    *   TV shows rich people as heroes.
    *   We can see a car, but we cannot "see" kindness easily.
4.  **Phenomena (What we see):**
    *   People posting photos of luxury holidays on Instagram.
    *   Respecting a rude rich man more than a polite waiter.
5.  **Effects (On psychology):**
    *   Constant jealousy and unhappiness.
    *   People go into debt to look rich.
6.  **Solutions (How to change):**
    *   Stop following "Influencers" who show off wealth.
    *   Praise achievements in charity, not just business.
7.  **Examples (Concrete instances):**
    *   **People** might treat a man in a suit better than a man in dirty work clothes.
    *   **Expensive cars** like Ferrari are seen as a sign of a "better" person.

### 💡 Argument 2: Society/Kindness (Old-Fashioned Values)
**Topic:** Kindness and honor are forgotten but essential.

1.  **Pros (Of Kindness):**
    *   It builds trust in the community.
    *   People help each other in times of trouble.
2.  **Cons (Of Losing Values):**
    *   Neighbors do not know each other's names.
    *   Society becomes cold and selfish.
3.  **Reasons (Why essential?):**
    *   Humans need connection to survive.
    *   Money cannot buy a true friend.
4.  **Phenomena (What we see):**
    *   Someone falling on the street and nobody helps.
    *   Old people living alone without visitors.
5.  **Effects (If values return):**
    *   Safer neighborhoods with less crime.
    *   Happier citizens who feel they belong.
6.  **Solutions (How to teach):**
    *   Parents must model kindness to their children.
    *   Schools should give awards for "Good Character".
7.  **Examples (Concrete instances):**
    *   **In small villages**, people leave their doors unlocked because they trust their neighbors.
    *   **A soldier** shows honor by protecting his country, even if the pay is low.

## ✏️ Essay 16 - Internet Problems

### 💡 Argument 1: Society/Crime (Online Theft)
**Topic:** Online crime (scams/theft) steals money.

1.  **Pros (Of Internet Banking - counter-point):**
    *   It is convenient to pay bills from home.
    *   You don't need to carry cash.
2.  **Cons (Of Cybercrime):**
    *   You can lose your life savings in one second.
    *   It is hard to catch criminals in other countries.
3.  **Reasons (Why it happens?):**
    *   Hackers are very smart and find weaknesses.
    *   People use weak passwords like "123456".
4.  **Phenomena (What we see):**
    *   Fake emails asking for your bank details (Phishing).
    *   News stories about data leaks.
5.  **Effects (On victims):**
    *   Financial ruin and bankruptcy.
    *   Loss of trust in online shopping.
6.  **Solutions (How to fix):**
    *   Use "Two-Factor Authentication" (password + phone code).
    *   Banks should refund money if it was stolen.
7.  **Examples (Concrete instances):**
    *   **An old man** lost $5000 because he clicked a fake link in an email.
    *   **Hackers** stole credit card numbers from millions of PlayStation users.

### 💡 Argument 2: Health/Bullying (Cyberbullying)
**Topic:** Cyberbullying hurts mental health.

1.  **Pros (Of Social Media - counter-point):**
    *   You can stay in touch with distant friends.
    *   You can share your hobbies.
2.  **Cons (Of Bullying):**
    *   Mean comments stay online forever.
    *   Bullies can hide their names (anonymity).
3.  **Reasons (Why bully?):**
    *   It is easier to be mean when you don't see the person's face.
    *   Jealousy of other people's "perfect" photos.
4.  **Phenomena (What we see):**
    *   Teenagers crying because of nasty comments on a photo.
    *   Hate groups targeting one student.
5.  **Effects (On health):**
    *   Depression, anxiety, and even suicide.
    *   Children refusing to go to school.
6.  **Solutions (How to fix):**
    *   Social media apps must delete bullying posts immediately.
    *   Block and report any user who is mean.
7.  **Examples (Concrete instances):**
    *   **A famous Singer** quit Instagram because people wrote hateful comments about her weight.
    *   **School children** create a group chat just to make fun of one classmate.
