## ✏️ Essay 17 — Living Alone Trend

**題目：** In some countries, many more people are choosing to live alone nowadays than in the past. Do you think this is a positive or negative development?

**選用 Ideas：**
1. **Health/Well-being (Mind/Happiness):** Freedom and less stress.
2. **Money/Economy (Spending):** People living alone spend more money (good for business).

**Essay:**

Many people today choose to live alone instead of with family or roommates. I think this is a positive development because it gives people freedom and helps the economy.

First, living alone is good for mental health. When people live with others, they often argue about cleaning or noise. This causes stress. Living alone gives a person complete freedom. **For example, a person can paint their walls any color they like or listen to music late at night without asking anyone.** This independence brings happiness and peace of mind.

Second, this trend is good for the economy. When a person lives alone, they need to buy their own furniture and appliances. They do not share these things. **For instance, they must buy their own washing machine, refrigerator, and TV for their new apartment.** This means they spend more money in shops, which creates jobs and helps businesses grow.

In conclusion, living alone is a good change. It reduces stress for the individual and increases spending for the economy.

## ✏️ Essay 18 — Wealthy Countries and Refugees

**題目：** Wealthy countries should accept more refugees and provide them with basic assistance such as food and housing. To what extent do you agree or disagree?

**選用 Ideas：**
1. **Society/Relationship (Humanity/Equality):** Rich countries should help the poor and safe lives.
2. **Money/Economy (Jobs/Labor):** Refugees can work and pay taxes.

**Essay:**

Should rich countries help refugees with food and housing? I completely agree with this idea. It is the right thing to do for humanity, and it can also help the economy.

First, accepting refugees is a moral duty. These people are running away from war and danger. They have lost their homes and are often hungry. **For example, giving a warm bed and hot food to a family who escaped a war zone saves them from sickness and death.** If we believe in equality and kindness, we must offer them safety to protect their lives.

Second, refugees can help the economy in the long run. Most refugees want to work hard to build a new life. If they get a job, they will pay taxes to the government. **For instance, many immigrants open small businesses like restaurants or repair shops that serve the local community.** These new citizens add value to the country and help it grow.

In conclusion, I support helping refugees. It is an act of kindness that saves lives, and these new citizens can contribute to the economy in the future.

## ✏️ Essay 19 — Government Funding for Artists

**題目：** Governments give lots of support to artists, even though some people think it is a waste of money that could have been spent better elsewhere. Discuss both views and give your opinion.

**選用 Ideas：**
1. **Society/Relationship (Culture):** Art is important for culture.
2. **Health/Well-being (Safety/Health):** Money should go to hospitals to save lives.

**Essay:**

Governments often give money to artists. Some people like this, but others think it is a waste. I believe that while art is nice, public money should be spent on more important things like healthcare.

On the one hand, art is part of our culture. Theatres and museums make our cities beautiful and interesting. If the government supports artists, they can create things that make people happy. **For example, a free music concert in a public park can bring the whole community together to celebrate.** This adds color to our society and improves our mood.

On the other hand, there are more urgent needs. Many hospitals do not have enough money. They lack doctors, nurses, and medicine. **For instance, the money spent on one large painting could be used to buy a new ambulance to save dying patients.** Saving a life is more important than art. Therefore, government spending should focus on health and safety first.

In conclusion, although art helps culture, I think the government should spend its limited money on hospitals to protect the health of its citizens.

## ✏️ Essay 20 — Cars in Developing Countries

**題目：** More and more people in developing countries are purchasing cars for the first time. What problems does this cause? What do you think are some possible solutions?

**選用 Ideas：**
1. **Society/Relationship (Environment):** Cars cause air pollution.
2. **Health/Well-being (Body):** Dirty air hurts health.
3. **Society/Relationship (Public Transport):** Government should build trains/buses.

**Essay:**

In developing countries, many people are buying cars now. This is a sign of wealth, but it causes serious problems. The main problem is pollution, and the solution is better public transport.

The increase in cars is bad for the environment and health. Old cars in developing countries often produce a lot of dirty smoke. This causes air pollution in cities. **For example, the black smoke from thousands of cars in a capital city can make the air very hard to breathe.** This is dangerous for the human body and can cause lung diseases for the people living there.

To solve this, the government must act. They should invest money to build good Public Transport, like trains and buses. **For instance, building a modern subway system can move millions of people to work quickly without creating any smoke.** If the bus system is cheap and fast, people will choose to ride it instead of driving.

In conclusion, more cars mean more pollution and health problems. However, investing in public transport can solve this issue and improve the environment.

## ✏️ Essay 21 — Teenagers and Smartphones

**題目：** Many teenagers have their own smartphones. Does this situation have more advantages or more disadvantages?

**選用 Ideas：**
1. **Health/Well-being (Safety):** Parents can find children (GPS).
2. **Society/Relationship (Communication):** Kids stop talking face-to-face.

**Essay:**

Most teenagers today have a smartphone. While this has some benefits, I believe the disadvantages are greater because it hurts social skills.

The main advantage is safety. Smartphones allow parents to contact their children at any time. If a teenager is in danger, they can call for help. Also, parents can use GPS to see where their children are. **For example, if a girl is walking home late at night, she can share her live location with her mother.** This gives peace of mind to the family.

However, the disadvantage is that teenagers talk less to people in real life. They spend hours looking at screens, texting, or playing games. **For instance, at a family dinner, children might look at their phones instead of talking to their parents about their day.** This is bad for their relationships. They might lose the ability to communicate well face-to-face.

In conclusion, although smartphones improve safety, I think the negative effect on social communication is a bigger problem for teenagers.

## ✏️ Essay 22 — Climate Change vs Nuclear Weapons

**題目：** Climate change is the biggest threat to life on our planet. How accurate is this statement? Are there any other big threats to mankind?

**選用 Ideas：**
1. **Society/Relationship (Environment):** Climate change is bad, but technology can fix it.
2. **Health/Well-being (Safety/Death):** Nuclear war kills everyone instantly.

**Essay:**

Some people say climate change is the biggest threat to Earth. I think it is a major problem, but nuclear weapons are an even bigger threat.

Climate change is definitely dangerous for our environment. It causes hot weather and storms. However, we have technology to solve this. **For example, solar panels and wind farms are already replacing dirty coal factories in many countries.** If we work together, we can fix the climate problem over time. It is a slow threat that we can manage.

In contrast, nuclear weapons are a fast and deadly threat. If countries go to war and use nuclear bombs, millions of people will die in a few minutes. **For instance, a single modern nuclear bomb can destroy a whole city the size of New York instantly.** The explosions would make the air poisonous and end human civilization. There is no technology to fix this damage.

In conclusion, while climate change is serious, I believe nuclear weapons are the biggest threat to mankind because they can destroy our safety and life instantly.
